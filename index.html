About Me

I am a young-adult professional looking to take on intriguing projects!

Welcome! I graduated from Vanderbilt in 2020 with my Bachelor's in Engineering Science with a focus on Management and Computing. Since then, I have been engaged in leading non-profit endeavors to enhance mental health for all, through the National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI) and via my own community initiatives.

I love playing improvised electrifying melodies on my alto saxophone and folding elaborate intricate origami for luxury home decor; both serve flavorful chicken soup for the soul. I apply creativity to every project that spearhead to enhance impact and empower my peers. Shoot me a message to collaborate!

Art of Origami

Transforming Paper,
One Fold at a Time

Printer paper itself may seem uninspiring to the naked eye. Yet, applying just a bit of time and energy can transform that 2D plane into some of the most fascinating creations, like romantic roses, serenading spirals, or intricate icosahedrons. Like such, the reality of life itself is a sheet of paper ready to be transfigured into awe-inducing works of art.

Musical Movements

Transforming Recovery,
One Note at a Time

Playing my alto saxophone elicits tremendous joy in both my illustrious mind and serenity-seeking soul. In my local ecosystem, I share my music through performing at community gatherings among friends and also for people being helped at local in-patient behavioral health centers. I hope to empower, encourage, and enable others just one note at a time.
